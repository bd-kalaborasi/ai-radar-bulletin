### US government partially lifts Mythos 5 export restrictions

**Source:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/26/us-government-anthropic-claude-mythos5-ai.html · CNBC · 2026-06-26
**Verification:** T2 secondary · disclosure · policy-regulation / ai-for-business

Anthropic launched Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 on June 9, 2026. Three days later, the US government issued an export control directive requiring Anthropic to suspend access for any foreign national, citing a claimed jailbreak in Fable 5 — a narrow code-reading vulnerability that Anthropic described as present in competing models too. On June 26, Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick signed a letter finding that 'appropriate safeguards are in place' for Mythos 5 only, clearing it for deployment to roughly 100 approved companies and federal agencies, primarily cyber defenders and critical infrastructure operators. Fable 5 remains suspended as of June 28; Anthropic said it continues working toward full restoration. Three independent reporting threads surfaced during the suspension window: the New York Times cited on-record employees claiming political targeting of specific Anthropic individuals; the Wall Street Journal profiled the Anthropic researcher briefing the government on Mythos jailbreak risk; and WIRED reported the White House demanded jailbreak-resistance at a level practitioners say is technically infeasible industry-wide.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** Mythos 5 is Anthropic's cybersecurity-specialized model. The partial unblocking restores access for organizations already approved as trusted partners; all other enterprises remain locked out. The standoff has surfaced a policy framework question: what authority allows the government to unilaterally suspend a commercially deployed AI model, and under what process? Operators planning frontier Anthropic model deployments in sensitive sectors should monitor EO 14409's voluntary benchmarking framework — that process now governs the formal pathway for future model access reviews.

**Key claims:**
- Roughly 100 companies and federal agencies cleared for Mythos 5 → CNBC (T2 secondary, June 26)
- 'Appropriate safeguards are in place' — Lutnick letter quoted by CNN Business, Bloomberg, NBC News (T2 secondary, June 26)
- Fable 5 remains suspended → CNN Business (T2 secondary, June 26)
- Initial launch: June 9, 2026; suspension directive: June 12, 2026 → Anthropic primary statement (T2, June 12)

**Cross-references:**
- https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access (T2, Anthropic primary — covers June 12 suspension directive)
- https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/us-allows-trusted-partners-to-use-anthropic-s-mythos-5-ai-model (T2, corroborating)
- https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/us-government-gives-anthropic-green-light-limited-re-release-mythos-5-rcna352018 (T2, corroborating)
- https://fortune.com/2026/06/27/anthropic-mythos-5-ai-model-us-commerce-department-clearance-fable/ (T2, corroborating)

**Caveats:** Howard Lutnick's letter was not publicly released; all details sourced via CNBC, Bloomberg, and NBC News who reviewed or were briefed on it. Anthropic's counter-claim that the Fable 5 jailbreak technique appears in competing models has not been independently verified by a third-party security firm with public disclosure. ### Codex Remote reaches general availability on all ChatGPT plans

**Source:** https://help.openai.com/en/articles/6825453-chatgpt-release-notes · OpenAI · 2026-06-25
**Verification:** T2 verified · changelog · workflow-automation

OpenAI moved Codex Remote from limited preview to general availability across all ChatGPT plans on June 25, 2026. The feature lets users start or continue work on a connected Mac or Windows Codex session from the ChatGPT iPhone, iPad, or Android app, via authenticated one-to-one QR pairing between each mobile device and host. Remote users can review Codex progress, approve actions, and steer long-running tasks without sitting at the desktop. A DigitalOcean Droplet Workspace plugin released simultaneously enables Codex to provision a cloud Droplet, configure SSH access, and connect it as a remote workspace — removing the requirement for a locally running machine.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** Codex Remote extends long-running AI coding tasks beyond the desktop by letting any ChatGPT subscriber supervise from mobile. The DigitalOcean plugin lowers the hardware barrier — teams without a dedicated local machine can host persistent Codex environments in the cloud rather than on a personal laptop.

**Key claims:**
- GA on all plans; QR-paired device authentication — ChatGPT release notes (T2, June 25)
- DigitalOcean Droplet Workspace plugin — ChatGPT release notes (T2, June 25)

**Cross-references:**
- https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/14/openai-says-codex-is-coming-to-your-phone/ (T3, background context on mobile rollout)
- https://www.techradar.com/pro/a-new-rhythm-for-collaboration-is-emerging-openai-adds-remote-access-to-bring-codex-to-chatgpt-for-iphone-ipad-and-android (T3, corroborating)

**Caveats:** Codex Remote requires a running Mac, Windows host, or DigitalOcean Droplet; it does not make Codex serverless. Usage is billed under ChatGPT AI Credits per the June 1, 2026 token-based billing transition.

### Anthropic consolidates API rate limits across Claude models

**Source:** https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/api/rate-limits · Anthropic developer platform · 2026-06-26
**Verification:** T2 verified · changelog · dev-tools

Anthropic restructured its API usage tiers and rate limits on June 26, 2026. Claude Sonnet and Claude Haiku rate limits now match Claude Opus limits at every tier — previously, only Opus had higher throughput at upper tiers, creating a ceiling for teams scaling production workloads on the cost-efficient models. The prior tier structure was collapsed into three tiers: Start, Build, and Scale. Organizations either maintained existing limits or received higher ones; no existing account saw a downgrade.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** Teams running high-throughput pipelines on Sonnet or Haiku had previously encountered lower rate ceilings even when able to absorb more usage. The unified limits remove a friction point for scaling production pipelines on Claude without switching to Opus for throughput reasons alone — a meaningful cost difference for latency-sensitive workloads.

**Key claims:**
- Sonnet and Haiku rate limits match Opus at every tier; three tiers: Start, Build, Scale; no account downgraded — Anthropic developer platform update (T2, June 26)

**Caveats:** The update was sourced via releasebot.io aggregating the Anthropic developer platform changelog; the primary entry is in platform.claude.com rather than a standalone public blog post. Per-model, per-tier numerical limits were not published in a standalone document; verify current values at your account tier in platform.claude.com/settings/limits.

### OpenAI deploys updated dictation model in ChatGPT

**Source:** https://help.openai.com/en/articles/6825453-chatgpt-release-notes · OpenAI · 2026-06-26
**Verification:** T2 verified · changelog · productivity-ai

OpenAI rolled out a new speech-to-text model for ChatGPT dictation across all plans on June 26, 2026. In OpenAI's internal evaluation, word error rate was at least 10% lower for top languages tested compared to the previous production model. Gains were noted on Japanese, Korean, Chinese, Urdu, Vietnamese, accented English, and long-form Spanish, French, Italian, and Portuguese. The model handles noisy environments, quiet or whispered speech, mixed-language code-switching, and transcription of letter-number combinations better than its predecessor.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** For teams using ChatGPT dictation in workflows involving multilingual communication, field notes, or meeting capture — particularly in markets where English and local-language code-switching is common — the accuracy improvement reduces post-edit load. The improvement in accented English transcription is directly relevant for global customer-facing use cases.

**Key claims:**
- At least 10% lower WER for top languages; gains on listed languages — ChatGPT release notes, OpenAI (T2, June 26)

**Caveats:** The WER improvement is from OpenAI's internal evaluation, not an independent speech benchmark. No external replication of the WER measurement has been published. The label 'top languages' is a self-selected subset; performance on lower-resource languages was not disclosed.

### Claude Opus 4.7 fast mode deprecated, removal July 24

**Source:** https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/api/rate-limits · Anthropic developer platform · 2026-06-25
**Verification:** T2 verified · changelog · model-release

Anthropic announced on June 25, 2026 that fast mode for Claude Opus 4.7 is deprecated, with the API removing support on July 24, 2026. After July 24, any request to `claude-opus-4-7` with `speed: 'fast'` will return an error. Developers are directed to migrate to Claude Opus 4.8, where fast mode remains supported.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** Developers running production workflows that call `claude-opus-4-7` with fast mode have a 29-day window from the June 25 announcement to July 24 removal. Auditing API call logs for the `speed: 'fast'` parameter against `claude-opus-4-7` is the action item; updating the model ID to `claude-opus-4-8` is the fix.

**Key claims:**
- Deprecation announced June 25, 2026; API removal July 24, 2026; error on `speed: 'fast'` after removal — Anthropic developer platform (T2, June 25)

**Caveats:** Source accessed via Anthropic developer platform documentation and releasebot.io aggregator. No capability or pricing changes for Opus 4.8 fast mode were noted alongside this deprecation notice.
