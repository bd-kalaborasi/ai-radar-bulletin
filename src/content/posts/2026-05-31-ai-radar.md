### OpenAI Codex adds Computer Use on Windows and mobile initiation

**Source:** https://developers.openai.com/codex/changelog · OpenAI Developers · 2026-05-29
**Verification:** T2 verified · announcement · workflow-automation / dev-tools

OpenAI released Codex version 26.527 on May 29, 2026, adding Computer Use support on Windows. The capability allows Codex to see, click, and type within Windows desktop applications in the foreground while executing a task — the same interaction mode previously available on macOS. Remote initiation from iOS or Android via the ChatGPT mobile app is also now supported, allowing users to start or monitor Codex tasks on a Windows machine from a phone. On Windows, Computer Use runs on the active desktop and takes foreground control during execution; it cannot operate in the background while a concurrent Windows session is in use. Computer Use on Windows is not available in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland at launch.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** Developers and QA engineers on Windows can now direct Codex to autonomously navigate and operate any Windows application — executing test runs, filing bug reports, or reviewing app output — without requiring API access to the target application. The mobile initiation pathway extends these autonomous coding workflows to scenarios where the developer is away from the machine.

**Key claims:**
- Computer Use on Windows: shipped 2026-05-29 (version 26.527) → OpenAI Codex changelog (T2)
- Mobile initiation via ChatGPT iOS/Android: same version → OpenAI Codex changelog (T2)
- Unavailable in EEA, UK, Switzerland at launch → https://www.totalum.app/blog/codex-on-windows-totalum (T3)

**Cross-references:**
- https://the-decoder.com/openais-codex-can-now-operate-your-windows-pc-autonomously-hunting-bugs-and-testing-apps-on-its-own/ (T3, corroborating)
- https://www.neowin.net/news/openai-rolls-out-major-codex-for-windows-update-with-computer-use-and-mobile-access/ (T3, corroborating)

**Caveats:** Foreground-only on Windows — execution takes over the desktop and cannot run alongside a concurrent user session. EEA/UK/Switzerland restriction in effect at launch; no timeline for regional expansion announced. Codex access requires eligible plan; not available to all ChatGPT tiers.

### Microsoft Copilot Studio May 2026 update ships A2A communication GA and voice agents GA

**Source:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-copilot/blog/copilot-studio/new-and-improved-computer-using-agents-a-new-workflows-experience-and-real-time-voice-experiences/ · Microsoft Copilot Blog · 2026-05-26
**Verification:** T2 verified · announcement · workflow-automation

Microsoft published the Copilot Studio May 2026 update on May 26, confirming several generally-available capabilities. Agent-to-agent (A2A) communication is now generally available, allowing Copilot Studio agents to delegate tasks and coordinate work across systems without human handoff at each step. Real-time voice agents reached general availability in North America through Dynamics 365 Contact Center, with support for caller identification, question answering, and context-preserving handoffs between AI and human agents. An early-release redesigned workflows canvas introduces agent nodes that embed AI execution within deterministic workflow steps. Microsoft also notes an improved orchestrator in early release achieving approximately 20% higher evaluation performance and 50% lower net token consumption compared to the prior version. Computer-using agents — enabling agents to operate website and desktop UIs directly without API support — had reached general availability separately on May 13, 2026.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** A2A GA in Copilot Studio removes the need for custom orchestration code between agents in enterprise automation stacks built on Power Platform. Multi-agent flows — for example, a research agent delegating to an approval agent delegating to a data-entry agent — can now be assembled within Copilot Studio without external middleware.

**Key claims:**
- A2A communication GA: May 2026 update → Microsoft Copilot Blog (T2)
- Voice agents GA in North America (Dynamics 365 Contact Center): May 2026 update → Microsoft Copilot Blog (T2)
- Computer-using agents GA: 2026-05-13 → https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/copilot-studio-blog/computer-using-agents-in-microsoft-copilot-studio-are-now-generally-available/4519427 (T2)
- Orchestrator improvement: ~20% eval performance, ~50% net token reduction → Microsoft Copilot Blog (T2; vendor-measured, not independently replicated)

**Cross-references:**
- https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/copilot-studio-blog/computer-using-agents-in-microsoft-copilot-studio-are-now-generally-available/4519427 (T2, CUA GA May 13 detail)

**Caveats:** Orchestrator performance figures are vendor-measured. Voice agents GA restricted to North America at launch. A2A communication requires Power Platform licensing; not available in all SKUs. Redesigned workflows experience is early release, not GA.

### DeepSeek makes V4-Pro 75% price cut permanent at May 31 deadline

**Source:** https://api-docs.deepseek.com/quick_start/pricing · DeepSeek · effective 2026-05-31 (announced ~2026-05-22)
**Verification:** T2 verified · pricing announcement · model-release

DeepSeek's 75% promotional discount on V4-Pro API pricing became permanent on May 31, 2026, when the original promotion window was scheduled to close. The new list price is $0.435 per million input tokens and $0.87 per million output tokens — compared with the pre-promotion list prices of $1.74/M input and $3.48/M output. Cache-hit input tokens are priced at $0.00363/M. DeepSeek announced the permanence of the discount approximately May 22, 2026, ahead of the effective date. No changes were made to model capabilities or API availability alongside the pricing update.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** The permanent pricing locks DeepSeek V4-Pro as a cost-efficient option at scale for high-volume API workloads. For teams evaluating API cost baselines across providers — batch document processing, automated code review, long-horizon agent runs — the pricing floor established today is a stable planning input.

### EU access to Anthropic Mythos stalled as US blocks non-US sharing

**Source:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/mythos-ai-models-eu-talks-us.html · CNBC · 2026-05-29
**Verification:** T3 secondary · policy reporting · policy-regulation
**Tier nuance:** CNBC primary returned HTTP 403 from this environment. Claims corroborated via The Star (T3), Blockonomi (T3), CDT Europe AI Bulletin (T3), and the Insurance Journal (T3).

The European Commission sought access to Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview for security evaluation and testing, according to a May 29 CNBC report citing European Commission spokesperson Thomas Regnier. Anthropic told the Commission that the White House would need to authorize sharing Mythos with non-US entities, and the US administration opposed such access due to security concerns. Commission officials planned direct meetings with Anthropic to pursue access arrangements. In parallel, Anthropic stated on May 28 that it expects to bring Mythos-class models to all customers "in the coming weeks," which the Commission views as a potential alternative pathway. EU AI Office enforcement powers begin in August 2026; at least one Commission official cited that timeline as a fallback mechanism for compelling access.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** Informational only for most commercial teams — no immediate workflow leverage. For organizations in the EU conducting AI risk assessments, AI Act compliance reviews, or procuring AI tools for security-relevant workflows, the standoff signals that access to frontier cybersecurity-capable models may remain jurisdictionally restricted through at least Q3 2026, independent of whether those models reach general commercial availability.

**Key claims:**
- EU Commission sought Mythos access; Anthropic cited need for US authorization → CNBC May 29 (T2, primary unavailable from this environment; confirmed via T3 secondary sources)
- White House opposed Anthropic sharing Mythos with non-US entities → CNBC / The Star (T3 corroboration)
- EU AI Office enforcement powers begin August 2026 → CDT Europe AI Bulletin (T3)

**Cross-references:**
- https://www.thestar.com.my/tech/tech-news/2026/05/29/anthropic-plans-wide-release-of-mythos-level-ai-models-in-weeks (T3, corroborating)

Anthropic's Mythos wide-release announcement (see 2026-05-29 bulletin) may render the bilateral access dispute moot once general rollout proceeds — but no GA date has been committed.
