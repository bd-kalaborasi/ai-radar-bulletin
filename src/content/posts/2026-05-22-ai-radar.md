### Claude Code ships /code-review command, agent view, and Windows reliability fixes

**Source:** https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/releases · Anthropic · 2026-05-21
**Verification:** T2 verified · changelog · dev-tools
**Caveats:** GitHub release page displayed year 2025 for this version; version sequence (v2.1.147 follows Week 20 versions v2.1.139–142 released May 11–15, 2026) and independent aggregator data confirm 2026-05-21 as the actual date.

Claude Code v2.1.147 released on 2026-05-21 renames `/simplify` to `/code-review` with an optional effort level argument (e.g., `/code-review high`), and fixes auto mode so it no longer suppresses `AskUserQuestion` when a skill explicitly relies on it. The release also ships reliability improvements: Windows PowerShell failures with winget/Microsoft Store installations are fixed, full-screen strobing in background sessions on Windows Terminal is resolved, and clipboard writes no longer expose copied content in process arguments visible to EDR/SIEM telemetry.

An earlier May release (v2.1.139–142, week of May 11–15) introduced `claude agents` — a unified screen showing all live Claude Code sessions, their status, and what they are blocked on — along with `claude agents --json` for scripting integrations with tmux, status bars, and session pickers.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** The `/code-review` effort-level control and the `claude agents` session view are directly relevant for teams running multiple parallel Claude Code sessions on large codebases — both reduce the overhead of coordinating multi-agent code tasks.

**Cross-references:**
- https://releasebot.io/updates/anthropic/claude-code (T3, corroborating — version history)
- https://code.claude.com/docs/en/whats-new (T2, changelog)

### Anthropic projects first operating profit ($559M) as SpaceX S-1 discloses the compute deal behind it

**Source:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/anthropic-says-its-about-to-have-its-first-profitable-quarter/ · TechCrunch · 2026-05-20
**Verification:** T2 secondary · financial disclosure via investor briefing · ai-for-business
**Tier nuance:** Primary source is Wall Street Journal (paywalled); TechCrunch and CNBC cite confirmed investor briefing. SpaceX compute-deal figures come from public SEC S-1 filing (T1 for deal terms).
**COI:** Both Anthropic and SpaceX have financial interest in framing the compute arrangement favorably ahead of planned capital raises.

Anthropic informed investors in a funding-round briefing that it projects Q2 2026 revenue of $10.9 billion — a 130% increase from Q1's $4.8 billion — and its first-ever operating profit of $559 million. Growth drivers cited: enterprise adoption of Claude in coding and cybersecurity workflows, and improved inference cost management. The company cautioned that operating profitability may not hold through the second half of 2026 due to large compute costs scheduled to increase.

On the same day (2026-05-20), SpaceX filed its public S-1 and disclosed the terms of its compute agreement with Anthropic: $1.25 billion per month through May 2029, with payments reduced in May and June 2026 as the arrangement ramps. The deal covers more than 220,000 NVIDIA GB200 GPUs across Colossus 1 and 2, totaling over 300 megawatts of capacity. A 90-day exit clause applies to both parties. An independent analysis at wheresyoured.at argues that the Q2 profit is structurally the result of the promotional May–June pricing window on the compute deal and will not persist when the full $15 billion annual rate applies.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** The financial disclosure affects how organizations should evaluate Anthropic's long-term stability as a vendor. The $10.9B Q2 revenue run rate signals strong enterprise demand for Claude; the compute-cost trajectory is the offsetting risk. For teams selecting frontier-model providers for multi-year contracts, the H2 compute ramp and upcoming IPO process are the relevant monitoring signals.

**Key claims:**
- Q2 revenue: $10.9B (+130% from Q1 $4.8B) → WSJ via TechCrunch, investor briefing (T2 secondary)
- Q2 operating profit: $559M → WSJ via CryptoBriefing (T2 secondary)
- Compute deal: $1.25B/month through May 2029 → SpaceX S-1 SEC filing (T1)
- GPU count: 220,000+ NVIDIA GB200s, 300MW+ → Axios reporting on S-1 (T2)
- May–June payments reduced as ramp-up → Axios (T2)

### White House AI executive order signing postponed by Trump

**Source:** https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/05/21/white-house-tore-down-ai-rules-now-its-building-new-defenses/ · Washington Post · 2026-05-21
**Verification:** T2 verified · news report · policy-regulation

President Trump postponed the signing of a frontier AI executive order on 2026-05-21, hours before the planned ceremony. The order would have established a voluntary 90-day pre-launch review framework for frontier AI models with NSA involvement in classified security testing. Trump told reporters he disliked certain aspects of the order, citing concern that it could impede US AI leadership relative to China. Invitations had already been sent to executives from leading AI companies before the postponement. No revised signing date has been announced.

**Why it matters for automation/productivity:** The postponement means there is currently no federal pre-launch review requirement for frontier AI models in the US. Organizations building AI governance programs around anticipated federal frameworks should treat those frameworks as still uncertain. Watch for a revised order; the NSA testing component was the element most likely to affect which models can be used in government-adjacent deployments.

**Key claims:**
- Voluntary 90-day pre-launch review framework with NSA involvement → WaPo (T2)
- Postponed hours before planned ceremony → WaPo, Bloomberg, CNBC (T2, multiple corroborating)

### Amazon commits $33 billion to AI and cloud infrastructure across Southeast Asia through 2039

**Verification:** T2 verified · announcement · ai-for-business

Amazon announced on 2026-05-21 that it plans to invest over $33 billion across Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, and Thailand through 2039, focused on AWS cloud and AI infrastructure including data centers. The company projects that these investments will contribute over $64 billion to the collective GDP of those four countries and support more than 56,300 full-time equivalent jobs annually in the data center supply chain. **Why it matters for automation/productivity:** For organizations in SEA evaluating AWS as an AI infrastructure provider, this signals a long-term regional commitment with physical data center presence. The Indonesia renewable energy deal is a notable first for that market and may affect enterprise sustainability reporting for AWS workloads.
